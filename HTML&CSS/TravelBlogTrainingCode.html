I'm Chris, I'm starting this website as both a practice for my HTML, CSS and Javascript skills, as well as a way to write down and record all the fantastic times I've had with my partner, Rebecca. Recently we returned from a trip to the USA.
We decided the best way to lay it all out is day by day and there's 16 of 'em!
The days kind of bleed into each other. Reason for this is every trip would start in one country/state and end in another. As exhausting as it was confusing.
Time travel is involved a lot in this. Not in a weird Dr Who way, just normal timezone shenanigans. What did you think I meant?

Now that I have your attention, let's move on to the adventure itself. We booked a holiday in September 2017 after only being together for about 4 months.

Christ, that could have backfired. Obviously it didn't. phew!.

Anyway, we booked the holiday for a great price and it came around before we knew it. It turned into an amazing adventure that I wouldn't have wanted to spend with anyone else.

Day 0: Manchester and Snow

The day before flying to New York was probably the best day one could have before any holiday.

It was snowing so badly I couldn't get my car off the estate and had to work from home. Mother nature, you take care of me so good.

Becca used this time to fight her way to the shops and get the last bits and bobs, whilst I stayed home and worked my buns off.

Before we knew it, we were packed and on the train from Durham to Manchester for a night in a hotel ready for the 9am flight. I love hotel showers. Feeling quite nervous about flying at this point but I resolve to not let my fears stop me from living how I want to.

Day 1: Flying, New Jersey and wow!

Early start. 5:30am. It's hard because we didn't even get to sleep until past 12. My grumbling doesn't last long though, we're both too excited.

The good news is Becca has stopped worrying so much about everything. We've had such conflicting approaches to this holiday. I'm happy to wing it (which is basically my attitude to life as a whole), whereas she likes to have all the details worked out. This meant a lot of panic in the run up to this trip.

Still, all sorted now. She can relax.

A quick uber ride has us at the airport where after a fancy breakfast at Frankie and Benny's, I find out that I don't like mimosas. Before we know it we're strapped in on the plane and have taken off, with me visibly sweating and shaking.
